Anxiety

Anxiety is a natural and normal response to stress or perceived threats. It is a feeling of apprehension or fear about what's to come, and it can be accompanied by physical sensations like increased heart rate, muscle tension, and restlessness. While anxiety is a common human experience, it becomes a concern when it is excessive, persistent, and interferes with daily life.

There are different types of anxiety disorders, each with its own specific symptoms and characteristics. Some common anxiety disorders include:

  • General Anxiety Disorder

    • In GAD, individuals experience excessive worry and anxiety about a wide range of everyday events and situations. The worry is often disproportionate to the actual threat.

  • Social Anxiety Disorder

    • People with social anxiety disorder feel intense fear and anxiety in social situations. They may fear judgment, embarrassment, or negative evaluation by others.

  • Panic Disorder

    • Panic disorder is characterized by recurrent panic attacks, which are sudden and intense periods of fear accompanied by physical symptoms like shortness of breath, chest pain, and dizziness.

  • Specific Phobias

    • Specific phobias involve intense fear and avoidance of a particular object, situation, or activity. Common phobias include fear of heights, spiders, flying, and enclosed spaces.
