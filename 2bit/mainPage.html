What is Sex Education?

Sex education is a broad term that encompasses the teaching and learning of various aspects of human sexuality, reproductive health, and relationships. Sex education typically covers anatomy, reproductive systems, contraception, sexually transmitted infections (STIs), consent, healthy relationships, and other related subjects. Comprehensive sex education is considered importantfor promoting informed decision-making, reducing the risk of unplanned pregnancies and STIs, and fostering healthy relationships.

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how individuals think, feel, and act, influencing their ability to handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood.This involves understanding and managing one's emotions effectively,as well as being able to cope with life's challenges.This includes cognitive functions such as problem-solving, decision-making, and memory.
